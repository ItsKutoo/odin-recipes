Bread

How to make bread

For this recipe I'm going to show you how to make the lightest, fluffiest bread out there.

Ingredients

  • 500g white flour, extra is optional for dusting
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 7g sachet of fast-action yeast
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 300ml water

Steps

  1. Mix the 500g white flour, 2 tsp salt and the 7g sachet of fast-action yeast in a large bowl.

  2. Make a well in the centre, then add 3 tbsp olive oil and 300ml water, and mix

    mix well. If the dough seems a little stiff, add another 1-2 tbsp water and mix.

  3. Lightly flour a clean surface and knead for about 10 minutes.

  4. Once its satin-smooth, place it in a lightly oiled bowl and cover with cling film, leave to rise for an hour until it had doubled in size, or you can place in the fridge overnight.

  5. Line a baking tray with parchment paper. Knock back the dough which is punching the air out and pulling the dough back in on itself, then gently mould it into a ball

  6. Place it on the parchement paper to sit for another hour until its doubled in size.

  7. Pre-heat oven to 220C/fan 200C/gas 7.

  8. Dust the loaf with flour if you want and cut a cross about 6cm long into the top of the loaf with a sharp knife.

  9. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown and the loaf sounds hallow when tapped underneath. Leave to cool on a wire rack and you have your bread.
